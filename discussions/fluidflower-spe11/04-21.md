- **21.04.2026, 14:30–15:45; Participants:** Ballini, Both, Elsheikh, Flemisch, Hajibeygi, Heinlein, Kane, Kumar, Lechevallier, Sadeghnejad  

- Two datasets were discussed: **SPE11** (well-defined, relatively rich; 34 submissions, ~200 snapshots with 8 variables) and **FluidFlower** (two experimental setups with different geology; sparse data, ~200×400 resolution, half-hour snapshots)

- Key ML questions:
  - FluidFlower: can models trained on one setup generalize to the other?
  - SPE11: can simulations be accelerated (though not ideal for benchmarking)?

- General points:
  - Need more datasets and setups, ideally in a hierarchical framework
  - Additional simulation data could be generated if needed

- FluidFlower:
  - Consider patch-based learning instead of full images
  - Possibly leverage ideas from weather forecasting
  - Use calibrated simulation models as ground truth
  - Previous history matching required many simulations; code/data may be reusable

- SPE11:
  - Question whether to train on a single model
  - Learn distributions to capture uncertainty
  - Consider decomposing the system into smaller components
