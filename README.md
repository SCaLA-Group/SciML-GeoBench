# Benchmarking for Scientific Machine Learning in Subsurface Geoscience

This repository accompanies the Lorentz Center workshop
["Benchmarking for Scientific Machine Learning in Subsurface Geoscience"](https://www.lorentzcenter.nl/benchmarking-for-scientific-machine-learning-in-subsurface-geoscience.html),
held on 20-24 April 2026 at Lorentz Center@omega in Leiden, the Netherlands.

The workshop brings together method developers and geoscience experts from
academia and industry to close the gap between SciML research and real-world
subsurface applications. A central goal is to define, implement, and evaluate
realistic benchmark problems that move step by step from academic toy settings
towards practical case studies.

Topics of interest include benchmark design for applications such as:

- subsurface heat transport
- earthquake prediction
- CO2 storage

The workshop emphasizes hands-on sessions and collaborative discussions over a
talk-heavy format. The resulting benchmarks are intended to be openly
accessible and reproducible, including benchmark definitions, datasets,
reference solutions, and evaluation metrics.
